7 Mountains in South Sulawesi

The mountains in South Sulawesi are non-volcanic mountains.

Fort Rotterdam

This fort was originally built in 1545 by the 9th King of Gowa named Daeng Matanre Karaeng Tumapa'risi' Kallonna. The site was then handed over to the Dutch VOC under the Bungaya Treaty of 1667 for occupation. This fort has six bastions and is surrounded by a seven meter high wall and a two meter deep moat.

Lolai To Tombi

Lolai To Tombi The Land Above the Clouds is located in Lembang (Village) Benteng Mamullu, Kapalapitu District, North Toraja Regency, South Sulawesi Province. Visitors can enjoy wisps of clouds from a height at Lolai To Tombi Land Above the Clouds or To Tomb

Lejja

Lejja is the name of a natural HOT WATER bathing place located in Watangsoppeng Regency, which leads to the route from Sidrap Regency through Batu-Batu subdistrict.

Apparalang Beach

The beach is a tourist spot that is suitable to visit, especially during the summer.

Bantimurung National Park

Bantimurung National Park is famous for its stunning limestone hills, lush tropical forests, and vibrant butterfly species, earning it the nickname "Kingdom of Butterflies." The park's beautiful waterfalls and caves make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike.
